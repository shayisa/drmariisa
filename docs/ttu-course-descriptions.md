## Program Context

Dr. Isa's courses form the core of TTU's **Forensic Anthropology Concentration** within the Anthropology B.A. program. The concentration requires five 3-hour courses (15 hours) with a grade of C or better:

- **Required Core (3 courses):** ANTH 3303, ANTH 3314, ANTH 4343
- **Elective (choose 1):** ANTH 3350 or ANTH 4320
- **Elective (choose 1):** FSCI 2308, ANTH 3350, ANTH 4320, GIST 3300, or GIST 3301

The anthropology major with a forensic anthropology concentration requires a total of at least **34 hours** of anthropology courses. ANTH 3314 is a prerequisite for ANTH 4343.

*Source: https://www.depts.ttu.edu/sasw/Anthropology/Anthropology_Undergrad.php*

## Undergraduate Courses

### ANTH 3303 — Forensic Anthropology
- **Credit Hours:** 3
- **Level:** Undergraduate
- **Prerequisites:** ANTH 2100, ANTH 2300 (recommended)
- **Years Offered by Dr. Isa:** 2020–Present
- **Role in Program:** Required core course for the Forensic Anthropology Concentration. Also counts toward the Forensic Sciences undergraduate minor (Department of Environmental Toxicology).
- **Catalog Title:** Forensic Anthropology

**Description (from NotebookLM research overview):**
Application of biological anthropology to legal contexts. Introduction to the methods of human identification, forensic case analysis, and the role of the forensic anthropologist in death investigation. Students learn the fundamentals of how biological anthropology is applied within legal and medicolegal contexts.

**Topics Likely Covered (based on standard forensic anthropology curricula and Dr. Isa's research areas):**
- History and scope of forensic anthropology
- The medicolegal death investigation system
- Role of the forensic anthropologist (lab vs. field)
- Human vs. non-human bone identification
- Biological profile: age, sex, ancestry, stature estimation
- Forensic taphonomy (postmortem changes)
- Trauma analysis overview (blunt, sharp, projectile)
- Personal identification methods
- Legal and ethical considerations
- Case studies and report writing

**Cross-listed/Related:** Counts toward the TTU Forensic Sciences Minor (Environmental Toxicology Dept.).

*Sources: TTU Anthropology Undergrad Program page, TTU Environmental Toxicology Minor page, NotebookLM overview*

### ANTH 3314 — Human Osteology
- **Credit Hours:** 3
- **Level:** Undergraduate
- **Prerequisites:** ANTH 2100, ANTH 2300
- **Years Offered by Dr. Isa:** 2022
- **Role in Program:** Required core course for the Forensic Anthropology Concentration. Prerequisite for ANTH 4343.
- **Catalog Title:** Human Osteology
- **Also Taught By:** Dr. Arthur Durband (per Uloop course notes listings)

**Description (from NotebookLM research overview):**
Foundational knowledge of skeletal biology and bone identification. A comprehensive survey of human skeletal anatomy emphasizing identification, functional morphology, and biological variation through intensive hands-on laboratory analysis.

**Topics Likely Covered:**
- Overview of the human skeleton and basic bone biology
- Anatomical terminology and orientation
- Anatomy of the skull (cranium, mandible, dentition)
- Postcranial anatomy (vertebral column, thorax, upper limb, lower limb)
- Bone identification: whole elements and fragmentary remains
- Side determination (left vs. right)
- Common anatomical variations
- Human vs. non-human bone differentiation
- Skeletal maturation and age-related changes
- Lab practicals with real skeletal material

*Sources: TTU Catalog (prerequisites confirmed), Uloop course notes listing, NotebookLM overview*

- Taphonomic assessment at recovery scenes

### ANTH 4343 — Methods in Forensic Anthropology
- **Credit Hours:** 3
- **Level:** Undergraduate
- **Prerequisites:** ANTH 2300, ANTH 2100, and ANTH 3314 (or instructor consent)
- **Years Offered by Dr. Isa:** 2021–Present
- **Role in Program:** Required core course for the Forensic Anthropology Concentration.
- **Catalog Title:** Human Skeletal Biology and Forensic Techniques (former title; now listed as Methods in Forensic Anthropology)
- **Also Taught By:** Dr. Robert Paine (per Uloop)

**Description (from Coursicle + NotebookLM):**
Intensive study of forensic anthropology methods including trauma analysis, age, sex, and stature estimation. Advanced training in biological profile estimation, trauma analysis, and taphonomic assessment using medicolegal casework methodologies and standardized reporting protocols.

**Topics Likely Covered:**
- Advanced methods of biological profile estimation
- Age estimation methods (auricular surface, pubic symphysis, rib phase, dental)
- Sex estimation methods (pelvis, skull, postcranial metrics)
- Ancestry estimation methods (craniometrics, morphoscopic traits)
- Stature estimation (regression formulae, measured bone lengths)
- Trauma analysis: blunt force, sharp force, projectile trauma
- Perimortem vs. postmortem fracture differentiation
- Taphonomic assessment
- Medicolegal casework methodology
- Standardized reporting protocols
- Forensic fractography concepts (Dr. Isa's specialty)

**Known Course Materials (from Uloop):**
- "The Scope of Forensic Anthropology" lecture/post
- Online activities (Activity 1, Activity 3)

*Sources: Coursicle (description confirmed), TTU Catalog (prerequisites confirmed), Uloop (course materials listed), NotebookLM overview, TTU Environmental Toxicology page (former title confirmed as "Human Skeletal Biology and Forensic Techniques")*

## Graduate Courses

### ANTH 5319 — Topics in Biological Anthropology: Skeletal Trauma & Taphonomy
- **Credit Hours:** 3
- **Level:** Graduate
- **Prerequisites:** Graduate standing (M.A. program enrollment)
- **Years Offered by Dr. Isa:** 2022–Present
- **Catalog Title:** Topics in Biological Anthropology (rotating topics course)

**Description (from NotebookLM research overview + website):**
Mechanistic analysis of trauma and postmortem changes. Advanced seminar on perimortem vs. postmortem fracture differentiation, ballistic trauma interpretation, and environmental modification of skeletal remains. This course directly reflects Dr. Isa's research specialization in bone biomechanics and fractography.

**Topics Likely Covered:**
- Bone as a biomechanical material (material science principles)
- Fracture mechanics: initiation, propagation, and termination
- Blunt force trauma: cranial and postcranial
- Sharp force trauma identification
- Projectile (gunshot) trauma analysis
- Forensic fractography: bone mirror, hackle, arrest lines, Wallner lines
- Perimortem vs. postmortem fracture characteristics
- Taphonomic processes and their effects on skeletal remains
- Decomposition stages and environmental factors
- Thermal alteration of bone
- Case studies using experimental research data
- Critical reading of current literature

**Connection to Research:** This course directly integrates Dr. Isa's experimental research on cranial fracture mechanics, long bone fractography, and the FBI Guide to Forensic Fractography of Bone (2022). Students learn the biomechanical framework published in her peer-reviewed articles.

*Sources: NotebookLM overview, TTU Graduate Program page, TTU Anthropology website*

### ANTH 5319 — Topics in Biological Anthropology: Cold Cases in Forensic Anthropology
- **Credit Hours:** 3
- **Level:** Graduate
- **Prerequisites:** Graduate standing
- **Years Offered by Dr. Isa:** 2022–Present
- **Catalog Title:** Topics in Biological Anthropology (rotating topics course)

**Description (from NotebookLM research overview + website):**
Interdisciplinary strategies for resolving long-term unidentified remains cases. Integrates skeletal re-analysis, forensic genetic genealogy, updated forensic art, and community-engaged investigation methods. The course explores how modern tools and collaborative approaches can resolve decades-old cases.

**Topics Likely Covered:**
- Landscape of unidentified persons in the U.S. (NamUs database)
- Cold case investigation methodologies
- Skeletal re-analysis with modern methods
- Forensic genetic genealogy (FGG) applications
- Updated forensic art and facial approximation
- Inclusive identification strategies (COSAGE framework)
- Structural vulnerability and identification barriers
- Interdisciplinary collaboration (law enforcement, genealogists, advocates)
- Community-engaged investigation methods
- Ethical considerations in cold case work
- Case studies (e.g., 103-year-old homicide case — Michael et al. 2022)
- Trans Doe Task Force collaboration model

**Connection to Research:** Directly draws on Dr. Isa's work with the Trans Doe Task Force, the COSAGE guide, and the 103-year-old homicide case resolution published in Forensic Sciences Research (2022).

*Sources: NotebookLM overview, TTU Graduate Program page, TTU Anthropology website*

## Curriculum Structure Notes

### Forensic Anthropology Concentration Prerequisites
- **ANTH 2100** — Introduction to Biological Anthropology (recommended for all advanced courses)
- **ANTH 2300** — Introduction to Cultural Anthropology (recommended for all advanced courses)
- **ANTH 2301** — Introduction to Archaeology (prerequisite for ANTH 4320)
- **ANTH 3314** is a prerequisite for **ANTH 4343**

### Graduate Program Requirements
The Anthropology M.A. program requires 9 hours of core courses across three subfields:
- **ANTH 5305** — Ethnology core
- **ANTH 5341** — Archaeology core
- **ANTH 5312** — Biological anthropology core

Students then specialize through elective courses (like Dr. Isa's ANTH 5319 topics courses) and thesis research. Dr. Isa's faculty profile lists her research areas as: "Human osteology, forensic anthropology, skeletal trauma analysis, bone biomechanics, personal identification."
