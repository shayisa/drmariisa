## THE PROMPT

Design a professional, multi-page academic portfolio website for a forensic anthropologist at a major research university. The site should feel like a high-end editorial experience — the kind of website that would impress academic peers, law enforcement partners, prospective graduate students, and university leadership alike.

**About the person:**
Dr. Mariyam (Mari) Isa is an Assistant Professor of Anthropology at Texas Tech University and Director of the Anthropology Research & IDentification (ARID) Laboratory. She holds a PhD from Michigan State University funded by a National Science Foundation Graduate Research Fellowship. She co-authored an FBI Laboratory publication on forensic fractography of bone. She serves as Executive Secretary of the NIST OSAC Forensic Anthropology Subcommittee and is a member of the AAFS Standards Board. She was selected as a 2025–2026 TTU Integrated Scholar. Her work spans experimental skeletal trauma research, inclusive identification of transgender and gender diverse individuals (the COSAGE guide), and forensic casework across 61 cases in 20+ counties.

**Site structure — 6 pages:**

1. **Homepage** — Hero with name, title, and a one-line mission statement. Below: a recognition banner (Integrated Scholar 2025–2026), a concise bio section, a statistics strip (12 peer-reviewed articles, 61 forensic cases, 20+ students mentored, 7 courses developed), a preview of key research areas (Forensic Fractography and Inclusive Identification), an "Organizations Engaged" strip showing logos/names of NIST OSAC, FBI, DPAA, and NYC OCME, and a contact call-to-action with email and "Join the Lab" buttons.

2. **Research** — A research portfolio page organized by pillars (not a chronological list). Three research pillars displayed as visual cards: Forensic Fractography & Biomechanics, Inclusive Identification (COSAGE), and Cold Case Resolution. Below: a featured section for the FBI Laboratory publication "A Guide to Forensic Fractography of Bone" with a pull quote. Then: Selected Publications (12 articles listed with journal name, year, and co-authors), Books & Chapters (4 entries), Research Reports (2 entries), Conference Activity summary (35 presentations), and Research Funding table.

3. **ARID Lab** — A dedicated lab hub page. Hero with lab name and tripartite mission: research, training, and casework. Casework statistics (61 cases, 20+ counties, active since 2014). Four service categories listed as cards: Medicolegal Significance, Biological Profile, Trauma Analysis, Search & Recovery. Affiliated Faculty section with 4 team members (name + role). A "Join the Lab" section with application window info and contact email.

4. **Teaching** — Opens with a verified quote: "The field of forensic anthropology can and should conceptualize professional ethical behavior beyond so-called scientific objectivity and justice beyond the court system." Outstanding Faculty Mentor Award (2022) featured prominently. Course listing for 7 TTU courses and 2 MSU courses (code, name, level, years). Student Mentorship section: 20+ students mentored, thesis committees chaired, undergraduate research outcomes with specific publications and conference presentations.

5. **Service & Impact** — Professional leadership roles (NIST OSAC Executive Secretary, AAFS Standards Board, conference co-organizer). Workshops & invited lectures (NYC OCME, DPAA, Texas State, Odessa PD, Michigan State Police). Advocacy section highlighting Trans Doe Task Force collaboration and the COSAGE guide. Archaeological fieldwork across Italy (Grosseto, Lecce, Civitella Paganico), Belize, and Michigan. University service roles. Peer review for journals and grant agencies. Full casework record summary.

6. **Privacy Policy** — Standard privacy policy page, linked from footer only.

**Key UX features to design for:**

- **Floating Table of Contents (CRITICAL):** A site-wide navigation panel that works across all pages. On desktop: a fixed sidebar on the right side that lists all site pages with expandable section links underneath each page. Uses scrollspy to highlight the active section as the user scrolls. Can be minimized to a slim vertical label hugging the right edge. Hidden while the hero section is visible, appears once the user scrolls past it. On mobile: a floating circular pill button in the bottom-right corner that opens a bottom-sheet overlay with the full site navigation. The TOC should use a subtle glassmorphic style (slight blur, semi-transparent background).

- **Responsive design:** Every page must work beautifully on desktop (widescreen), tablet, and mobile. Use a clean grid system with generous whitespace.

- **Dark mode support:** Design with both light and dark color schemes. The dark mode should feel scholarly and elegant, not just an inverted palette.

**Visual direction:**

- Aesthetic: Scholarly editorial — think academic journal meets modern portfolio. Clean, confident, authoritative. Not flashy or corporate.
- Typography: A refined serif for headings (conveying academic authority) paired with a clean sans-serif for body text (conveying approachability).
- Color palette: Warm, muted tones — think aged paper, deep earth tones, subtle sage or olive accents. Avoid bright primary colors. The palette should feel serious and grounded, befitting forensic science.
- Layout: Generous whitespace. No visual clutter. Let the content breathe. Use tonal background shifts between sections rather than hard borders or dividers.
- Cards and containers: Subtle, with soft shadows or tonal differentiation. No heavy borders.
- Photography placeholders: Include placeholder areas for a professional portrait (hero), lab/fieldwork photos (ARID Lab page), and a texture or pattern element that evokes bone structure or archaeological layers.
- Stats and numbers: Display key metrics prominently using large typography — these are impact indicators that visitors scan for immediately.

**What NOT to do:**
- Do not make it look like a startup landing page or a tech product site.
- Do not use bright gradients, neon accents, or playful illustrations.
- Do not use a dark-only design — light mode is the primary experience.
- Do not crowd the layout — this person's work is deep, and the design should let each section stand on its own.
