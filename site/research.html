Experimental Investigations of Blunt Force Trauma in the Human Skeleton

NSF GRFP-funded doctoral research and NIJ-funded collaborative work investigating fracture initiation and propagation in cranial, mandibular, and long bone impacts using controlled experiments, high-speed videography, and fractographic analysis of bone failure surfaces.

COSAGE: Contextual Observations in Support of All Gender Expressions

A crowdsourced biocultural coding guide developed to document physical and contextual evidence relevant to personal identification and mitigate cognitive bias in forensic identification. Partnered with the Trans Doe Task Force and State Medical Examiners to modernize contextual coding in unidentified remains cases.

Status Active — Multi-Site Validation
Collaborative Team Trans Doe Task Force, State Medical Examiners
Cold Case Resolution Interdisciplinary Collaboration

Reinvestigating Historical Unidentified Remains

Applying modern forensic genetic genealogy and skeletal analysis to resolve long-standing cold cases, including the 1992 Ina Jane Doe case (correcting over-pathologized identity narratives) and the 1916 Dubois Cave case (integrating genealogical and osteological evidence).

Status Ongoing — Multiple Cases

Scientific Abstract

"This manual details the fractographic analysis of human bone, integrating principles of material science to decipher impact mechanics. We explore the relationship between strain rate, extrinsic toughening mechanisms, and the resultant surface features (e.g., arrest lines, hackle) observed in peri-mortem trauma."

Public Impact Summary

"Bones break in predictable ways depending on how they are hit. This guide teaches investigators how to look at a broken bone and work backward to understand the weapon used or the height of a fall. By standardizing these signs, we help law enforcement provide more accurate evidence in criminal cases."

Forensic Fractography & Biomechanics

A Guide to Forensic Fractography of Bone

FBI Laboratory

Christensen, Isa, Smith, Hefner, Berryman, Saginor, & Webb (2022). Laboratory Division of the FBI, No. 21.35.

A practitioner-oriented guide to applying fractographic analysis to skeletal fractures — teaching forensic anthropologists to use fracture surface features (bone mirror, hackle, arrest lines, Wallner lines) to determine fracture origins and crack propagation direction in blunt and projectile trauma.

Access Publication →

Bullet Mass and Velocity: Fracture Patterns in Flat Bones

Journal of Forensic Sciences

Edwards* & Isa (2026). Journal of Forensic Sciences.

Investigated relationships between bullet mass, velocity, kinetic energy, and fracture propagation in flat bones using controlled experiments firing 9mm bullets of different masses at pig scapulae, with fracture characteristics documented using standard fractographic terminology.

Access Publication →

Projectile Kinetic Energy and Firearm-to-Target Distance on Fracture Behavior

Journal of Forensic Sciences

Edwards* & Isa (2024). Journal of Forensic Sciences, 69(2), 400-414.

Used a fractographic approach to investigate the relationships between muzzle kinetic energy, firearm-to-target distance, and fracture morphology in gunshot trauma. Gunshot experiments on pig scapulae using three firearms generating different kinetic energies provided data on the presence, quantity, and length of fracture characteristics.

Access Publication →

Input Energy and Impactor Shape on Cranial Fracture Patterns

Forensic Science International

Isa, Fenton, Goots, Watson, Vaughan, & Wei (2023). Forensic Science International, 352, 111859.

Explored the effects of input energy and impactor shape on cranial fracture patterns in blunt human head impacts. Research showed that input energy is a primary determinant of fracture severity and crack propagation, and that different impactor shapes produce distinct fracture patterns at identical energy levels.

Access Publication →

Cranial Fracture Initiation in Blunt Human Head Impacts

Forensic Science International

Isa, Fenton, Goots, Watson, Vaughan, & Wei (2019). Forensic Science International, 300, 51-62.

Used high-speed video to capture cranial fracture initiation and propagation in impact experiments on unembalmed human cadaver heads, providing direct evidence that fractures can initiate at locations remote from the impact site and propagate back toward the point of contact.

Access Publication →

Impact Direction in 3-Point Bending of Human Femora

Journal of Forensic Sciences

Isa, Fenton, DeLand, & Haut (2018). Journal of Forensic Sciences, 63(1), 38-46.

Examined incomplete butterfly fractures in human femora under 3-point bending, showing that the wedge fragment does not contain the fracture origin and that crack propagation direction can be assessed from fractographic surface features on the complementary bone surfaces.

Access Publication →

Reverse Butterfly Fractures: Experimental Fractography

Forensic Science International

Isa, Fenton, Antonelli, Vaughan, & Wei (2021). Forensic Science International, 325, 110899.

Investigated how "reverse" butterfly fractures form — unconventional fractures where the transverse portion forms on the tension side and the wedge on the compression side. Using concentrated 4-point bending experiments on long bones, the study explored how complex loading conditions and variations in bone geometry lead to reverse wedge orientations.

Access Publication →

Blunt Force Trauma in the Human Mandible

Forensic Sci. Int. Reports

Goots, Isa, Fenton, & Wei (2021). Forensic Science International Reports, 5(5), 100252.

Investigated blunt force impacts delivered to five specific locations on the human mandible — ramus, posterior body, and mid-body — to determine how known impact points correlate with resulting fracture patterns, establishing classification rates and diagnostic criteria for expert testimony regarding facial trauma.

Access Publication →

Chest Radiograph Comparison for Decomposed Remains

Journal of Forensic Sciences

Isa, Hefner, & Markey (2017). Journal of Forensic Sciences, 62(5), 1304-1307.

Tested whether a chest radiograph comparison method originally designed for dry skeletal elements could be applied to semifleshed, decomposing human remains — successfully demonstrating its use for positive identification of decedents in advanced stages of decomposition.

Inclusive Identification

Introducing COSAGE: Documenting Gender Expression in Human Identification

Am. J. Biological Anthropology

Blatt, Isa, Michael, Flaherty, Smith, Howard, & Unwin (2025). American Journal of Biological Anthropology, 188:e70180.

Introduced the COSAGE framework — a four-part biocultural tool for systematically documenting skeletal sex estimates alongside evidence of medical care and contextual factors, directly aiding the inclusive identification of underrepresented decedent populations.

Shifting the Forensic Anthropological Paradigm for TGD Communities

Humans

Adams, Blatt, Flaherty, Haug, Isa, Michael, & Smith (2023). Humans, 3, 142-165.

Identified systemic barriers within cultural and medicolegal institutions that impede the identification of underrepresented persons, and provided actionable strategies for inclusive casework, research, and education.

Redefining Ethical Considerations in Forensic Anthropology

American Anthropologist

Adams, Goldstein, Isa, Kim, Moore, Pilloud, & Tallman (2022). American Anthropologist, 124(3), 597-612.

Challenged the traditional paradigm that prioritizes strict scientific objectivity as an absolute ideal, establishing that advocacy, activism, and antiracism are essential ethical responsibilities for forensic anthropologists working with marginalized communities.

Solving a 103-Year-Old Homicide with Forensic Anthropology & Genetic Genealogy

Forensic Sciences Research

Michael, Blatt, Isa, Redgrave, & Ubelaker (2022). Forensic Sciences Research, 7(3), 412-426.

Demonstrated the successful identification of a 1916 murder victim (Joseph Henry Loveless) through the integration of forensic genetic genealogy with skeletal re-analysis — proving the power of combining interdisciplinary methods to resolve century-old cold cases.
