Pedagogy & Purpose

Nurturing the Next Generation of Forensic Scientists

My teaching philosophy is built on the pillars of critical inquiry, ethical responsibility, and hands-on discovery. I believe that forensic anthropology is not just a collection of techniques, but a profound commitment to justice and human dignity.

I aim to transform the classroom into a collaborative laboratory where students bridge the gap between skeletal biology and its social implications.

Curriculum Excellence

Bridging anatomical precision with real-world investigative rigor through specialized forensic coursework.

7 TTU Courses · 2 MSU Courses

Human Osteology

ANTH 3314 · Undergraduate

A comprehensive survey of human skeletal anatomy emphasizing identification, functional morphology, and biological variation through intensive hands-on laboratory analysis.

Methods in Forensic Anthropology

ANTH 4343 · Undergraduate

Advanced training in biological profile estimation, trauma analysis, and taphonomic assessment using medicolegal casework methodologies and standardized reporting protocols. Prerequisite: ANTH 3314 Human Osteology.

Skeletal Trauma & Taphonomy

ANTH 5319 · Graduate

Advanced seminar on the mechanistic analysis of perimortem vs. postmortem fracture differentiation, ballistic trauma interpretation, and environmental modification of skeletal remains.

Forensic Anthropology

ANTH 3303 · Undergraduate

Introduction to the application of biological anthropology within legal and medicolegal contexts, covering methods of human identification, forensic case analysis, and the role of the forensic anthropologist in death investigation.

Forensic Archaeology

ANTH 4320 · Undergraduate

Training in archaeological techniques applied to forensic contexts — systematic search, recovery, and documentation of human remains and associated evidence at outdoor and buried scenes.

Cold Cases in Forensic Anthropology

ANTH 5319 · Graduate

Interdisciplinary strategies for resolving long-term unidentified remains cases, integrating skeletal re-analysis, forensic genetic genealogy, updated forensic art, and community-engaged investigation methods.

Human Osteology (Graduate)

ANTH 5319 · Graduate

Graduate-level skeletal biology and bone identification, building on foundational osteological knowledge with advanced analytical methods for research and casework applications.

Michigan State University · 2016–2019

Human Osteology & Forensic Anthropology

ANP 441 · Undergraduate

Undergraduate course integrating human skeletal anatomy with forensic anthropological methods, taught at Michigan State University in 2019.

Women & Health

ANP 270 · Undergraduate

Undergraduate anthropological examination of health through a gendered lens, exploring biocultural perspectives on health disparities. Taught at Michigan State University in 2016.
