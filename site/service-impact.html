Advocacy & Community

Justice & Inclusion
handshake

Trans Doe Task Force

An ongoing collaboration since 2020 uniting the osteological and sociocultural knowledge of forensic anthropologists with the experiential knowledge of LGBTQ+ advocates and genealogists to develop gender-expansive strategies for identifying transgender and gender-diverse victims in cold cases.

format_quote

"The field of forensic anthropology can and should conceptualize professional ethical behavior beyond so-called scientific objectivity and justice beyond the court system."

— Dr. Mari Isa, American Anthropologist (2022)

video_camera_front

TRANScending Jane & John Doe

Six-Hour Webinar · March 2021

Co-organized with Idaho State University's Gender Resource Center and the Trans Doe Task Force to educate law enforcement on gender-informed cold case strategies, forensic genetic genealogy, and harm reduction in TGD identification.

database

LAMMP Database

Presented at AAFS · 2022

Co-presented on the LGBT+ Accountability for Missing and Murdered Persons database, highlighting the limitations of traditional missing persons databases that often misgender or "deadname" victims, impeding identification efforts.

Transqat Podcast

Discussed the identification of transgender and gender-diverse individuals in forensic contexts, the ethics of objectivity in science, and the structural vulnerability framework for understanding anti-transgender violence.
